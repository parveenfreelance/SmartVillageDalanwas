Village History

Dalanwas has a rich and proud history. The village settlement journey began from Pitampura (Delhi), then moved to Chandaliya, and finally reached its present location.

In Samvat 1454, the village was established by Daluram. His son Madararam, belonging to the Parpara (Parvati) clan, had ancestral roots in Dobda village, Rajasthan.

In Samvat 1452, a local woman became Kuwari Sati, symbolizing supreme sacrifice and devotion.

Spiritual & Religious Heritage

A native saint, Savant (Savantia), attained spiritual greatness through deep meditation and penance. He is worshipped as Shri Shri 1008 Siddh Shri Baba Sawanth Nath.

Temples dedicated to Baba Sawanth Nath are located in Salas, Bhirod, and Pralnwas, attracting devotees from across the region. Baba took Samadhi in Dalanwas, making the village a sacred spiritual center.

Social Harmony

Dalanwas is a symbol of unity and brotherhood. Communities including Jats, Brahmins, Sunars, Nais, Khatis, Kumhars, Chhippis, Jogis, Dhanaks, and Chamars live together peacefully, respecting each other‚Äôs traditions and values.

Education & Infrastructure

The village is equipped with essential facilities such as GGS School, Sports Ground, Public Library, Fawara Chowk, and Pidhiya Dham, contributing to education, recreation, and social development.

Pride & Achievements

Late Colonel Peerdan remains a source of immense pride for the village. Even today, many residents serve as Colonels, Majors, IPS officers, and have been honored with military medals.

Numerous villagers serve in the Indian Army, Haryana Police, Delhi Police, education sector, and other government services.

Agriculture & Livelihood

Farmers of Dalanwas earn their livelihood through livestock rearing and vegetable farming. Many villagers are also engaged in private jobs, independent businesses, and overseas employment with reputed organizations.

Our Vision

While preserving its traditions and values, Dalanwas continues to move forward with a vision of education, self-reliance, unity, and sustainable development for future generations.
