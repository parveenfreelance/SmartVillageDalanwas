ЁЯПЫя╕П Village History

Dalanwas has a rich and proud history. The village settlement journey began from Pitampura (Delhi), then moved to Chandaliya, and finally reached its present location.

In Samvat 1454, the village was established by Daluram. His son Madararam, belonging to a girl from the Parvati clan, whose village is Dobda in Rajasthan, became a unmarried Sati..

In Samvat 1464, Parvati became Kuwari Sati, symbolizing supreme sacrifice and devotion.

ЁЯХЙя╕П Spiritual & Religious Heritage

A native saint, Savant (Savantia), attained spiritual greatness through deep meditation and penance. He is worshipped as Shri Shri 1008 Siddh Shri Baba Sawanth Nath.

Temples dedicated to Baba Sawanth Nath are located in Salas, Bhirod, and Pralnwas, attracting devotees from across the region. Baba took Samadhi in Dalanwas, making the village a sacred spiritual center.

ЁЯдЭ Social Harmony

Dalanwas is a symbol of unity and brotherhood. Communities including Jats, Brahmins, Sunars, Nais, Khatis, Kumhars, Chhippis, Jogis, Dhanaks, and Chamars live together peacefully, respecting each otherтАЩs traditions and values.

ЁЯОУ Education & Infrastructure

The village is equipped with essential facilities such as GGS School, Sports Ground, Public Library, Fawara Chowk, contributing to education, recreation, and social development.

ЁЯПЕ Pride & Achievements

Late Colonel Peerdan Lamba remains a source of immense pride for the village. Even today, many residents serve as Colonels, Majors, IPS officers, and have been honored with military medals.

Numerous villagers serve in the Indian Army, Haryana Police, Delhi Police, education sector, and other government services.

ЁЯМ╛ Agriculture & Livelihood

Farmers of Dalanwas earn their livelihood through livestock rearing and vegetable farming. Many villagers are also engaged in private jobs, independent businesses, and overseas employment with reputed organizations.

ЁЯМН Our Vision

While preserving its traditions and values, Dalanwas continues to move forward with a vision of education, self-reliance, unity, and sustainable development for future generations.

ЁЯТз Pila Johad

Pila Johad is a traditional water reservoir near Dalanwas, known for its unique healing legend. It is believed that people suffering from jaundice who bathe in the Johad recover from the disease, but the miracle only works for visitors from outside the village.

The Johad is surrounded by lush greenery and serves as a serene spot for nature lovers. The area also features beautiful Sani and Shiva temples, where devotees come to pray and seek blessings.

This combination of spiritual significance, natural beauty, and local legends makes Pila Johad an important and unique place for visitors and pilgrims alike.

ЁЯЩП Dadi Sati Mela, Dalanwas

The Dadi Sati Mela is held in Dalanwas (Mahendragarh, Haryana) after Holi (Dulendi) and is an important local festival. People who do not celebrate Holi instead worship Dadi Sati, who is similar to Rani Sati from Rajasthan and revered far and wide, especially during the month of Bhadon.

Location: Dalanwas and Gadadwas villages, Mahendragarh district, Haryana.
Timing: After Holi, on Dulendi day, Holi is not celebrated here, but the Dadi Sati Mela is organized.

Story of Rani Sati Dadi: This fair is dedicated to Rani Sati Dadi from Rajasthan, a woman who performed Sati after her husband's death. She was born as Narayani and is associated with the Mahabharata era, symbolizing devotion and dedication.

Main Attractions: The fair is grand, with devotees visiting the temples of Dadi Ji. Cultural events such as wrestling (dangal) and other local festivities are also organized.

In summary, the Dadi Sati Mela of Dalanwas is a local religious festival held after Holi, celebrated in memory of Rani Sati Dadi, worshipped in both Haryana and Rajasthan regions.
